By 2 Years Old, My Child Will Likely...

Physical Development:

  • Walk, run and climb without help.
  • Go up and down stairs with little help.
  • Take steps backwards.
  • Like to run but can't always stop and turn well.
  • Can stack two to four blocks.
  • Help wash hands.
  • Bend over to pick up a toy without falling.
  • Drink with a straw.
  • Feed himself with a spoon.
  • Toss or rolls a large ball.
  • Enjoy sitting on and moving small-wheeled riding toys.
  • Open cabinets, drawers and boxes.
  • Begin to gain some control of bowels and bladder, but complete control may not be achieved until around age 3. (Boys often do not complete toilet training until age 3 ½)

Social & Emotional Development:

  • Like to imitate parents' actions.
  • Begin to show signs of independence.
  • Love to use the word "no."
  • Have difficulty remembering rules.
  • Enjoy exploring and gets into everything.
  • Need constant attention.
  • Have difficulty sharing.
  • Be very possessive.
  • Try to do many things independently.
  • Find it difficult to wait and wants things right now!
  • Get angry sometimes and have temper tantrums.
  • At times get physically aggressive when frustrated.
  • At times slap or hit.
  • Act shy around strangers.
  • Refer to self by name.
  • Use the words "me" and "mine."
  • Learn that anger and despair pass.

Intellectual Development:

  • Have a rapidly growing vocabulary including names of toys.
  • Use 2 and 3 word sentences.
  • Use about 50 or more words frequently.
  • Echo single words that are spoken by someone else.
  • Talk to himself and jabbers expressively.
  • Use the words "please" and "thank you" if prompted.
  • Show preferences between toys.
  • Like to choose between two objects.
  • Enjoy singing songs or saying rhymes.
  • Enjoy looking at picture books.
  • Like simple pretend play such as wearing hats and talking on the phone.
  • Resist sharing toys with other children.

Delevopmental Milestones Associated with Feeding:

  • Can use a straw.
  • Chew food with mouth closed and shifts food in mouth.
  • Distinguish between finger and spoon foods.
  • Hold small glass in one hand; replaces glass without dropping.
  • Use spoon correctly but with some spilling.

If you have concerns about your child’s development talk to your doctor or contact the Early Development Network.

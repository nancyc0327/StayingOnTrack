By 9 Months Old, My Child Will Likely...

Physical Development:

  • Reach for cup or spoon when being fed.
  • Drink from a cup with help.
  • Enjoy some finely-chopped solid foods.
  • Close mouth firmly or turn head when no longer hungry.
  • Sleep 11 to 13 hours at night.
  • Need 2 to 3 naps a day.
  • Develop a rhythm for feeding, eliminating and sleeping.
  • Have true eye color established.
  • Sit alone without support.
  • "Rake" objects with hand.
  • Transfer objects from one hand to another.
  • Have hair growth begin to cover head.
  • Crawl or scoot forward.
  • Begin to pull himself up and stand with help.

Social & Emotional Development:

  • Respond to own name.
  • Recognize family member's names.
  • Respond differently to family and strangers.
  • Become distressed if a toy is taken away.
  • Show fear of falling off high places (table, stairs).
  • Show mild to severe anxiety at separation from parent.
  • Imitate sounds, actions and facial expressions.
  • Raise arms as a sign to be held.
  • Like to be tickled and touched.

Intellectual Development:

  • Learn by using all five senses.
  • Make noises to show displeasure or satisfaction.
  • Focus eyes on small objects and reach for them.
  • Enjoy dropping objects over edge of chair or crib.
  • Love to dump toys or things out of containers.
  • Look for a ball rolled out of sight.
  • Babble as if talking.

Delevopmental Milestones Associated with Feeding:

  • Refuse food by keeping lips closed: has taste preferences.
  • Hold a spoon and plays with it during feeding.
  • May drink from a straw.
  • Drink from a cup with assistance.

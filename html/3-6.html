By 6 Months Old, My Child Will Likely...

Physical Development:

  • Try to grab with hand.
  • Wiggle and kick with arms and legs.
  • Roll over (stomach to back).
  • Sit with support.
  • Have teeth appear.
  • Have control of his head and arm movements.
  • Hold head up and look around.
  • Push himself up onto his hands and knees.

Social & Emotional Development:

  • Cry different ways for different reasons.
  • Love to be touched or held close.
  • Respond to a shaking rattle or bell and to peek-a-boo games.
  • Coo, giggle, and make lots of sounds.
  • Show signs of fear, anger or disgust.
  • Try to talk to image in mirror.
  • Recognize familiar faces and smile at them.
  • Search for sounds and turn his head towards them.
  • Make most vowel sounds and about half of the consonant sounds.

Intellectual Development:

  • Can use eyes and hands together.
  • Explore objects with mouth.
  • Recognize bottle or breast.
  • Understand he can reach out to tough objects.
  • Recognize his mother.
  • Distinguish between familiar people and strangers.
  • Expect feeding, dressing, and bathing.

Delevopmental Milestones Associated with Feeding:

  • Approximate lips to the rim of a cup.
  • Use fingers to feed self a cracker.
  • Chew and bite.
  • Hold own bottle, but may not drink from it (prefers for it to be held).
